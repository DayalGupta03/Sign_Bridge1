hey# 100 Essential ASL Signs – Detailed 5‑Point Format

Below are 100 essential ASL signs, each described in the exact 5‑point format you requested:

---

## 1. HELLO

SIGN NAME: HELLO

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Flat hand with all four fingers straight and held tightly together, thumb resting alongside the index finger so the hand forms a firm, flat “salute” shape.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing outward directly away from your body, fingers pointing upward, with the wrist straight and aligned with the forearm.

Motion:
- Start with the fingertips of the dominant hand near or just above your eyebrow on the side of your forehead, then move the hand outward and slightly forward in a small arc, like a casual salute that ends a short distance away from the head.

---

## 2. GOODBYE

SIGN NAME: GOODBYE

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Flat hand with all fingers straight and together, thumb relaxed at the side, as if you are about to wave.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing outward away from your body, fingers pointing generally upward, wrist in a neutral, comfortable position.

Motion:
- Raise the hand to about head or shoulder height in front of you and bend only the top finger joints so the fingers fold toward the palm and extend again, repeating this opening‑and‑closing motion or a gentle side‑to‑side wave 2–3 times.

---

## 3. PLEASE

SIGN NAME: PLEASE

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Flat hand with all fingers straight and together, thumb alongside the palm, so the hand forms a broad, smooth surface.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing inward directly toward the center of your chest, fingers pointing upward, wrist relaxed and straight.

Motion:
- Place the flat palm on the middle of your chest and move it in slow, smooth circles (usually clockwise from your perspective), keeping the contact light but continuous as the hand traces 2–3 full circles over your chest.

---

## 4. THANK YOU

SIGN NAME: THANK YOU

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Flat hand with all fingers straight and together, thumb alongside the fingers, forming a firm, even surface.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm angled slightly inward toward your chin or lips, fingers pointing up and out toward the person you are thanking.

Motion:
- Start with the fingertips lightly touching your chin or just below your lower lip, then move the hand forward and slightly downward in a smooth arc away from your face, ending at arm’s length as if sending the thanks outward.

---

## 5. SORRY

SIGN NAME: SORRY

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Closed fist with all fingers curled into the palm and the thumb resting on top or alongside the other fingers, forming a compact, rounded shape.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing inward against the upper chest, roughly over the heart area, wrist neutral.

Motion:
- Place the fist on your chest and rub it in small, slow circles (usually clockwise from your perspective), keeping the motion gentle and repeated 2–3 times while your facial expression shows regret or apology.

---

## 6. GOOD

SIGN NAME: GOOD

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Flat hand with all fingers straight and together, thumb alongside, creating a smooth, flat palm.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm first facing inward toward your chin or lips, then turning slightly upward as the hand moves out.

Motion:
- Touch the fingertips briefly to your chin or lips, then move the hand outward and slightly downward away from your face, as if you are taking something good from your mouth and offering it outward into the space in front of you.

---

## 7. BAD

SIGN NAME: BAD

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Flat hand with all fingers straight and together, thumb alongside the index finger, same basic shape as GOOD.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm starts facing inward toward the chin, then flips so the palm faces downward as the motion completes.

Motion:
- Begin with the fingertips touching your chin as in GOOD, but as you move the hand outward you sharply rotate the wrist so the palm ends facing downward, letting the hand drop slightly to suggest something falling or being discarded.

---

## 8. YES

SIGN NAME: YES

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Fist with all fingers curled into the palm and the thumb pressing over or alongside the fingers, forming a tight, rounded shape.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing sideways or slightly inward toward your body at chest level, wrist relaxed.

Motion:
- Bounce the fist up and down in a short, nodding motion—like a miniature head nod—moving from the wrist or elbow 2–3 times while your face shows agreement or affirmation.

---

## 9. NO

SIGN NAME: NO

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Index and middle fingers extended and held together, thumb extended to the side so the thumb and the two fingers can pinch together; remaining fingers curled into the palm.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm angled slightly toward the person you are addressing, fingers pointing upward or slightly outward.

Motion:
- Tap the tips of the index and middle fingers down toward the thumb 1–2 times, like a little “talking” beak closing, or make a small side‑to‑side shake with this handshape near chin level to strongly emphasize refusal.

---

## 10. I / ME

SIGN NAME: I / ME

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Index finger extended straight while the other fingers curl into the palm, thumb resting alongside the curled fingers.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing inward toward your chest, index finger pointing directly toward your torso, wrist straight.

Motion:
- Bring the extended index finger to touch or lightly tap the center of your chest once or twice, clearly indicating yourself as the subject.

---

## 11. YOU

SIGN NAME: YOU

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Index finger extended straight with the remaining fingers curled into the palm, thumb resting at the side.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm turned slightly upward or sideways, index finger pointing straight toward the person you are addressing.

Motion:
- Extend your arm a comfortable distance and aim the pointing index finger directly at the specific person, holding it steady for a moment to anchor them in the signing space.

---

## 12. HE / SHE / IT

SIGN NAME: HE / SHE / IT

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Index finger extended, other fingers curled into the palm, thumb relaxed at the side.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm slightly upward, index finger pointing toward the location in space associated with that person or object.

Motion:
- Point with the index finger toward a fixed spot in the air where you have “placed” that person or object in your signing space, using a brief, clear point without touching your own body.

---

## 13. WE / US

SIGN NAME: WE / US

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Index finger extended, other fingers curled into the palm.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing inward or slightly upward, index finger moving across your upper chest.

Motion:
- Touch or point with the index finger first near one side of your chest (over one shoulder), then arc the hand across to the opposite side of your chest, tapping again, to show a group that includes yourself and others.

---

## 14. THEY / THEM

SIGN NAME: THEY / THEM

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Index finger extended, remaining fingers curled, thumb relaxed at the side.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing slightly upward, index finger pointing outward toward an imagined line of people.

Motion:
- Sweep the pointing index finger in a short, horizontal arc in front of you, passing across the area where the group is mentally located, to indicate multiple people not including yourself.

---

## 15. MY / MINE

SIGN NAME: MY / MINE

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Flat hand with fingers straight and together, thumb alongside forming a broad surface.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing inward directly toward the center of your chest.

Motion:
- Place the flat palm firmly against the center of your chest and either hold it there or add a small push inward, clearly marking ownership or association with yourself.

---

## 16. YOUR / YOURS

SIGN NAME: YOUR / YOURS

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Flat hand with all fingers straight and together, thumb resting along the side.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing outward toward the person, fingers pointing upward, wrist straight.

Motion:
- Start with the flat palm a short distance from your chest and extend your arm so the hand moves straight forward toward the other person, as if presenting something in front of them to show it belongs to or concerns them.

---

## 17. WHO

SIGN NAME: WHO

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Index finger extended and slightly bent at the knuckle, other fingers curled into the palm with the thumb resting loosely.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm angled toward your face or slightly upward, hand held near your chin or mouth.

Motion:
- Position the bent index finger near your chin and repeatedly flex and straighten it in small movements, as if the finger is “hooking” inward, while maintaining a questioning facial expression.

---

## 18. WHAT

SIGN NAME: WHAT

Hands Used:
- Both hands, symmetric

Hand Shape:
- Both hands open with fingers relaxed and slightly spread (a loose 5‑handshape), thumbs extended comfortably.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palms facing upward toward the ceiling, wrists relaxed so the hands hang naturally in front of your body at about waist or lower chest height.

Motion:
- Gently wiggle or rock both hands side to side in small, quick motions, letting the fingers flutter slightly as if you are showing confusion or asking for clarification about “what.”

---

## 19. WHERE

SIGN NAME: WHERE

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Index finger extended straight up, other fingers curled into the palm, thumb resting along the side.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing outward or slightly inward, index finger pointing upward, hand held in front of you at about chest or face level.

Motion:
- Move the extended index finger side‑to‑side in small, quick motions, like a tiny windshield wiper, while your facial expression shows that you are searching for a location.

---

## 20. WHEN

SIGN NAME: WHEN

Hands Used:
- Both hands

Hand Shape:
- Both hands with index fingers extended, other fingers curled into the palms, thumbs resting alongside.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Non‑dominant index held upright in front of you, palm inward; dominant index starts touching or near the tip of the non‑dominant index.

Motion:
- Circle the dominant index finger around the non‑dominant index and bring it back to touch the same point, tracing a small loop, as if marking a particular moment on a timeline.

---

## 21. WHY

SIGN NAME: WHY

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Start from a flat hand at the side of the head and end in a "Y"‑like shape (thumb and pinky extended, other fingers curled) or an open hand with middle finger touching the temple then flicking out.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm begins facing your head near the side of your forehead or temple, then turns outward as it moves away.

Motion:
- Touch the side of your head with your fingers and then move the hand away from the head while changing into the "Y" shape or flicking the middle finger outward, expressing the idea of a thought turning into a question of “why.”

---

## 22. HOW

SIGN NAME: HOW

Hands Used:
- Both hands

Hand Shape:
- Both hands in curved or bent shapes (like loose fists with knuckles touching and fingers pointing downward), thumbs resting along the sides.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Knuckles of both hands touch or nearly touch in front of your body, palms facing each other and slightly downward.

Motion:
- Rotate the dominant hand (or both hands together) forward so the palm of that hand turns upward, like you are opening your hands to show something, while your face signals you are asking about the manner or method.

---

## 23. HAPPY

SIGN NAME: HAPPY

Hands Used:
- One or both hands (commonly both)

Hand Shape:
- Open hands with fingers straight and slightly spread (relaxed 5‑handshapes), thumbs relaxed.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palms facing inward toward the chest, hands positioned over the upper chest area.

Motion:
- Move one or both hands in small upward brushing or circular motions on the upper chest, as if positive feelings are rising up, while your face shows a broad smile and uplifted expression.

---

## 24. SAD

SIGN NAME: SAD

Hands Used:
- Both hands

Hand Shape:
- Open hands with fingers together or slightly apart, relaxed and straight.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palms facing inward toward your face, hands held in front of your face with fingers pointing upward.

Motion:
- Slide both hands downward slowly from eye level toward your chest, as if your feelings or facial features are drooping, and let your facial expression fall into a frown or downturned look.

---

## 25. ANGRY / MAD

SIGN NAME: ANGRY

Hands Used:
- One hand (dominant)

Hand Shape:
- Claw‑like hand with all fingers spread and sharply bent at the knuckles, thumb also bent, forming a tense, cupped shape.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palm facing inward toward your face or just in front of it, fingers pointing toward your nose or mouth.

Motion:
- Start with the claw hand near the lower face and move it sharply upward toward your nose or mouth in a short, jerky motion, letting your face tighten with a scowl to show anger boiling up.

---

## 26. TIRED

SIGN NAME: TIRED

Hands Used:
- Both hands

Hand Shape:
- Both hands in curved or "C"‑like shapes with fingers together and slightly bent, thumbs out to the side.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palms facing inward toward your chest, fingertips touching the upper chest near the collarbones.

Motion:
- With your shoulders slightly slumped, let your elbows drop and your hands slide downward a little from the chest so the hands tilt outward, as if your body is sagging from exhaustion.

---

## 27. SICK

SIGN NAME: SICK

Hands Used:
- Both hands

Hand Shape:
- Both hands with middle fingers extended slightly forward while the other fingers remain relaxed (middle fingers act as "contact" fingers).

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Dominant hand’s palm facing inward with the middle fingertip touching the forehead; non‑dominant hand’s palm inward with the middle fingertip touching the upper stomach.

Motion:
- Hold both hands so the middle fingers make light contact—one at the forehead and one at the stomach—then you can emphasize the sign by gently tapping or holding the contact points while your face shows discomfort or nausea.

---

## 28. HURT / PAIN

SIGN NAME: HURT / PAIN

Hands Used:
- Both hands, symmetric

Hand Shape:
- Both hands with only the index fingers extended and slightly pointed, other fingers tucked into the palms.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palms facing each other so both index fingertips aim toward a central point.

Motion:
- Position the extended index fingers a few inches apart at the location of the pain (for example in front of the head, chest, or generic space), then twist both hands in opposite directions while keeping the tips facing each other, repeating this inward twisting motion 2–3 times to show sharp or stabbing pain.

---

## 29. SCARED / AFRAID

Palm & Wrist Orientation:

Motion:
- Start with hands closer to your chest, then quickly move them outward a short distance while your shoulders jump or tense, as if startled, and your facial expression shows fear or surprise.

---

## 30. EXCITED

## 36. DRINK

## 37. SLEEP

Palm & Wrist Orientation:

## 38. AWAKE / WAKE UP

SIGN NAME: WAKE UP

- Both hands

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palms facing inward toward the eyes at the start, then turning slightly outward as the hands open.

Motion:
- Place closed hands near the corners of your closed eyes and then quickly open both hands into "L" or spread‑finger shapes while opening your eyes wide, as though you’re popping your eyes open in surprise or waking.

## 41. WALK

SIGN NAME: WALK

Hands Used:
- Both hands

Hand Shape:
- Both hands in "3"‑handshapes, with index and middle fingers extended and separated, and the thumb extended; remaining fingers curled into the palm.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palms facing downward toward the ground, “legs” (the two extended fingers) pointing forward.

Motion:
- Alternate moving the two "3"‑hands forward in a gentle, stepping pattern, like little legs walking across a surface, keeping the motion smooth and rhythmic.

## 42. RUN

SIGN NAME: RUN

Hands Used:
- Both hands

Hand Shape:
- Both hands in "L"‑ or "1"‑handshapes with the dominant thumb hooking around the index finger of the non‑dominant hand, forming a linked configuration.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Palms facing downward or slightly inward, hands held in front of the body.

Motion:
- With the fingers hooked, move the joined hands forward in quick, small repeated motions, as if the hands are pumping or jogging, to show the idea of running.

## 43. HELP

SIGN NAME: HELP

Hands Used:
- Both hands, different roles

Hand Shape:
- Non‑dominant: flat hand with fingers straight and together, palm up.
- Dominant: closed fist with thumb on top, resting on the non‑dominant palm.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Non‑dominant palm facing upward like a platform; dominant fist’s palm facing inward or slightly forward.

Motion:
- Place the dominant fist on the open non‑dominant palm and then lift both hands upward together a short distance, as if you are physically raising or supporting something, clearly expressing the idea of giving help.

---

## 44. STOP

- Non‑dominant: flat hand with fingers straight and together, palm up.

Palm & Wrist Orientation:
- Non‑dominant palm facing upward in front of your body; dominant palm facing sideways or downward as it comes down.

Motion:
- Hold the non‑dominant hand steady like a small table, then bring the edge of the dominant hand down sharply at a right angle onto the open palm once, creating a clear, abrupt stopping gesture.
